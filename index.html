Welcome to Telangana – The Land of Culture, Courage, and Celebration.

Telangana, India’s 29th and youngest state, was formed on June 2, 2014, with Hyderabad as its capital. Rich in history, tradition, and innovation, Telangana stands as a vibrant blend of cultural heritage and modern development. Nestled in the heart of the Deccan Plateau, the state is known for its distinct language, art forms, architecture, festivals, and cuisine, making it a truly unique region of India. Telangana’s historical legacy dates back to the Satavahanas, Kakatiyas, Qutub Shahis, and Asaf Jahis, leaving behind architectural marvels like the Charminar, Golconda Fort, Warangal Fort, and many ancient temples and stepwells. The state’s official language, Telugu, echoes through centuries of poetry, folklore, and classical literature. The people of Telangana are known for their warmth, resilience, and deep-rooted traditions. Festivals like Bathukamma and Bonalu celebrate the spirit of womanhood and community bonding through vibrant flowers, rituals, and dance. Telangana is also home to tribal cultures and craft traditions like Pochampally Ikat, Nirmal paintings, and Dokra art. Agriculture and IT both thrive here. While the fertile lands support crops like rice, cotton, and maize, cities like Hyderabad, Warangal, and Karimnagar have become hubs for technology, pharma, and innovation. Initiatives like T-Hub and T-Works reflect the state’s push toward a digital future. Whether you are exploring its spiritual sites, tasting its fiery cuisine, or engaging with its growing industries, Telangana welcomes you with open arms. Discover a state that’s proud of its roots and passionate about its future.
