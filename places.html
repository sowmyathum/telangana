See the Unseen, Feel the Untold – Explore Telangana    

Telangana is a land where time flows through ancient stones, lush landscapes, and vibrant streets. The state is known for its remarkable blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, offering travelers a chance to experience something truly unique. From majestic forts to serene lakes, from peaceful temples to bustling markets, every place in Telangana tells a story that resonates with tradition and pride. Whether you're an explorer seeking heritage, a nature lover looking for tranquility, or a curious traveler drawn to local life, Telangana’s destinations welcome you with warmth and wonder. The state’s landscapes shift from rocky plateaus to green forests, and every corner holds an invitation to pause, reflect, and connect. As you journey through Telangana, you’ll discover more than just places—you'll uncover the spirit of a land that honors its past while embracing the future.

The Thousand Pillar Temple is a renowned architectural marvel located in Hanamkonda, Warangal, Telangana. Built in 1163 AD by King Rudra Deva of the Kakatiya dynasty, the temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, Vishnu, and Surya, reflecting the region’s religious diversity. Its name comes from the presence of a multitude of intricately carved pillars, though not literally a thousand. The temple features a unique star-shaped (stellate) layout and is a classic example of a blend between Chalukyan and Dravidian architectural styles

A highlight is the monolithic Nandi sculpture carved from a single rock, positioned in front of the temple. The temple’s pillars, doorways, and ceilings are adorned with detailed geometric patterns and exquisite carvings, showcasing the advanced craftsmanship of Kakatiya sculptors. Despite damage during invasions, restoration efforts have preserved its historical and cultural significance, making it a major tourist and heritage site in Telangana



Ramoji Film City, located about 30 km from Hyderabad in Telangana, is the world's largest integrated film studio complex, certified by the Guinness World Records. Founded in 1996 by media entrepreneur Ramoji Rao, it spans over 2,000 acres of land, originally covered with forests and hills, which were preserved during its construction. Designed by renowned art director Nitish Roy, the film city was envisioned as a one-stop destination for filmmakers, providing all facilities from shooting sets to post-production.

The complex houses 47 soundstages, 50 studio floors, multiple outdoor film sets, and various production and post-production facilities. It has hosted numerous Indian and international films, including blockbusters like Baahubali and Krrish 3. Beyond filmmaking, Ramoji Film City is a major tourist attraction featuring amusement parks, live stunt shows, gardens, shopping areas, and themed attractions, drawing over 1.5 million visitors annually. It offers a unique blend of cinema magic and entertainment for all ages.



Yadadri, also known as Yadagirigutta, is a prominent pilgrimage town located about 60 km from Hyderabad in Telangana. It is famous for the Sri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple, dedicated to Lord Narasimha, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. The temple is perched on a hillock and attracts thousands of devotees year-round, especially during festivals like Narasimha Jayanti.

The temple’s history dates back several centuries, but it has recently undergone extensive renovation and expansion, transforming it into a grand architectural marvel blending traditional and modern styles. The temple complex features intricate carvings, spacious halls, and beautifully landscaped surroundings, enhancing the spiritual experience.

Yadadri holds immense religious significance for Vaishnavites and is considered a powerful spiritual center. The serene environment, coupled with the temple’s divine aura, makes it a must-visit destination for devotees and tourists alike. The Telangana government has developed the area to improve accessibility and facilities, promoting Yadadri as a key cultural and religious tourism spot.



Nagarjuna Sagar Dam is one of the largest and highest masonry dams in the world, located on the Krishna River between Nalgonda district of Telangana and Guntur district of Andhra Pradesh. Constructed between 1955 and 1967, it was inaugurated by Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru in 1955. The dam stands 124 meters (407 feet) tall and stretches about 1.6 kilometers (5,200 feet) long, featuring 26 crest gates for flood control.

The reservoir created by the dam has a gross storage capacity of approximately 11,472 million cubic meters (405 TMC), covering a water spread area of around 285 square kilometers. It provides irrigation water to several districts in both Telangana and Andhra Pradesh, supporting nearly 9.81 lakh acres of farmland.

Besides irrigation, Nagarjuna Sagar Dam is a significant source of hydroelectric power, with an installed capacity of 816 MW. The dam is a symbol of post-independence engineering achievement and plays a crucial role in regional agriculture, power generation, and water management. It is also a popular tourist destination due to its impressive scale and scenic surroundings.
